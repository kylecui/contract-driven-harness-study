# Product Decision Brief Template (Go / No-Go / Pivot)

## 1) Use case

Use this template when teams need an explicit decision, not a research recap.
A decision brief should be readable in one pass by leadership and operators.

## 2) Decision statement block

Start with one sentence:

"Decision requested: [Go / No-Go / Pivot] for [initiative] in [scope/timeframe]."

Add context:

- decision owner,
- decision deadline,
- affected teams,
- reversibility level (easy/medium/hard).

## 3) Evidence summary format

Summarize evidence by source class:

- user evidence,
- market/competitive evidence,
- validation experiment evidence,
- operational/financial evidence.

For each claim include:

- evidence ID or source link,
- confidence level,
- known contradiction or uncertainty.

Avoid unsupported adjectives like "strong" without confidence rationale.

## 4) Option set (minimum three)

Always include:

1. Recommended option
2. Conservative alternative
3. High-upside/high-risk alternative

For each option provide:

- expected upside,
- major risks,
- resource requirement,
- time-to-signal,
- dependency constraints.

## 5) Risk/reward framing

Use comparable dimensions across options.

Recommended dimensions:

- strategic impact,
- execution risk,
- financial exposure,
- reputational/compliance risk,
- reversibility.

Score each dimension (1-5) and explain highest two risk drivers.

## 6) Recommendation with conditions

Do not issue unconditional recommendations.

Template:

"Recommend [option] provided that [condition A, B, C] are met by [date/milestone]."

Conditions should be measurable and linked to evidence gaps.

## 7) Escape clauses (explicit)

Define circumstances to stop or re-route execution.

Escape clause template:

"If [metric/risk event] crosses [threshold], switch to [fallback option] within [time window]."

Examples:

- If activation stays below 25% after two validation cycles, pivot to assisted rollout model.
- If compliance review identifies high-severity blocker, no-go until remediation closes.

## 8) Confidence declaration

Separate conclusion from confidence.

Use two labels:

- decision confidence (low/medium/high),
- evidence completeness (partial/substantial/comprehensive).

State top unknowns that could change the decision.

## 9) Implementation gate plan

Define gated progression after decision:

- Gate 1: pre-launch readiness
- Gate 2: early signal check
- Gate 3: scale or rollback decision

Each gate should specify:

- owner,
- metrics,
- threshold,
- review date.

## 10) Brief layout (copy-ready)

## A. Decision requested

## B. Executive summary (5-7 bullets)

## C. Evidence summary table

## D. Option comparison

## E. Risk/reward assessment

## F. Recommendation and conditions

## G. Escape clauses and fallback plan

## H. Open risks and next validation tasks

## 11) Quality checklist before submission

- Recommendation is explicit and binary enough for action.
- Every major claim has evidence traceability.
- At least one meaningful alternative is evaluated fairly.
- Conditions and escape clauses are measurable.
- Confidence and uncertainty are declared without spin.

## 12) Example recommendation snippet

"Recommend Go for vertical-first rollout to Segment A only, conditioned on: onboarding completion >=40%, support burden <=1.5x baseline, and no unresolved high-severity compliance findings. If onboarding fails after two iterations, pivot to hybrid assisted deployment."
