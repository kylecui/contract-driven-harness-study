**Research Synthesis: Proposal Track Relationship Analysis**

---

### **Extracted Evidence**

**E1** – From `proposal_guidelines.md`:  
> “Track A focuses on early-stage, high-risk exploratory projects with a funding cap of $50k. Track B is for mature, near-deployment projects with a cap of $200k.”

**E2** – From `committee_minutes.txt`:  
> “Several reviewers noted that Track A proposals often lack clear evaluation plans, while Track B proposals sometimes include exploratory elements better suited to Track A.”

**E3** – From `feedback_summary.csv` (aggregated):  
> 42% of applicants reported difficulty choosing between Track A and Track B; 28% of Track B submissions were flagged as “too exploratory” by reviewers.

**E4** – From `program_director_interview.txt`:  
> “The original intent was to separate by stage, not by risk. But in practice, risk and stage are correlated. We’ve seen some Track A projects outgrow the $50k limit before completion.”

**E5** – From `success_metrics.pdf`:  
> Track A has a 15% conversion rate to follow-on funding; Track B has a 65% implementation rate within 18 months.

**E6** – From `committee_minutes.txt`:  
> “One suggestion was to merge tracks and use a phased funding approach: an exploratory phase (cap $50k) and a development phase (cap $150k).”

---

### **Inferences from Evidence**

- **Stage vs. Risk Ambiguity** (E1, E2, E4):  
  The design separates by project stage, but applicants and reviewers conflate stage with risk, leading to misalignment and misclassification.

- **Applicant Confusion** (E3):  
  A significant portion of applicants struggle to self-select into the correct track, suggesting unclear boundaries.

- **Funding Flexibility Gap** (E4):  
  Track A’s rigid cap may hinder projects that progress faster than expected, indicating a need for more adaptive funding.

- **Performance Difference** (E5):  
  Track B shows higher implementation readiness, but Track A serves as a necessary feeder for innovation, with measurable follow-on success.

- **Potential Solution Pathway** (E6):  
  A unified track with phased funding could preserve stage distinction while reducing misclassification and allowing in-project progression.

---

### **Unresolved Research Risks**

1. **Operational Risk**: Would a merged track increase reviewer burden or require new evaluation criteria?  
2. **Historical Data Risk**: Past success metrics (E5) are track-specific; predicting outcomes under a merged model lacks direct evidence.  
3. **Stakeholder Alignment Risk**: No evidence on whether the committee would support a structural change beyond the suggestion noted in E6.  

---

### **Evidence-Backed Decision**

Given the evidence of applicant confusion (E3), reviewer inconsistency (E2), and the potential for phased funding to address stage progression (E4, E6), **the two tracks should be combined into a single track with clear, phased milestones and adaptive funding limits**.  

**Supporting Evidence IDs**: E2, E3, E4, E6.
