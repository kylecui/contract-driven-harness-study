# Contract-Driven Harness Claim-Evaluation Map

## Purpose

This map constrains the paper draft. Each contribution claim must map to evaluation evidence and must preserve its boundary.

## Claims

| Claim ID | Draft contribution claim | Supporting evidence | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | Contract-rich harnessing improves absolute contract adherence in tested productivity slices. | Structured extraction, project initialization, and research-workflow slices; claim-boundary memo `P2-E70`. | Does not imply universal gap compression or production readiness. |
| C2 | Gap compression is real but conditional. | Structured extraction v2 compressed measured nonzero gaps; project initialization and research workflow were mixed or n/a. | Only report compression when G0 baseline gaps are nonzero and the absolute gap does not reopen in the opposite direction. |
| C3 | Mechanism atoms make broad workflow failures interpretable. | Mechanism atom definition, coverage framework, Stage 6-7 atom pilots. | Atom pass does not imply workflow pass. |
| C4 | A repair-loop protocol can convert specific low-cost-model failures into explicit, testable obligations. | Stage 7e v1-v4: `P2-E62` through `P2-E69`. | Fixed evidence-bound decision macro only. |
| C5 | Stage 7e v4 obligations transfer to a neighboring method-plan update macro. | Stage 7-next local and smoke: `P2-E72` through `P2-E75`. | Fixed-input, no-tool, deterministic method-plan macro only. |
| C6 | The current strongest claim is weak-model enablement on bounded contract-critical operations. | Stage 7r.1, Stage 7e v4, Stage 7-next smoke. | Does not show low-cost models are generally equivalent to strong models. |

## Non-Claims

| Non-claim | Reason |
|---|---|
| The harness universally closes model gaps. | Project initialization and research workflow results are mixed or n/a; gap compression depends on baseline gaps and relative gains. |
| The harness is production ready. | No production deployment, live tool execution, security review, or operational monitoring evidence is present. |
| Full project initialization is solved. | Earlier broad project-initialization slices were mixed; current passing macros are fixed-input and no-tool. |
| Full research workflow is solved. | Current evidence covers evidence-bound macros, not open-ended source discovery, tool use, or live synthesis workflows. |
| Atom success proves workflow success. | Stage 7p v1 showed composition can fail even when component mechanisms look promising. |

## Evaluation Coverage

| Evaluation block | What it proves | What it does not prove |
|---|---|---|
| Structured extraction v2 | High-constraint tasks can show gap compression under G9. | General workflow reliability. |

## Paper Position

The paper should be framed as a methodology and bounded empirical case study:

> Contract-driven harness engineering can externalize specific reliability obligations and enable low-cost models on bounded, contract-critical operations. Gap compression occurs under some task/metric conditions, but the more stable contribution is the mechanism-first repair loop and its claim-boundary discipline.
