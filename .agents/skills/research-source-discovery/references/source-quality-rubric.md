# Source Quality Rubric

## Quality Dimensions

### 1. Authority
| Rating | Criterion |
|---|---|
| High | Official spec, peer-reviewed paper, government publication, primary data |
| Medium | Reputable industry report, established blog, conference talk, tech report |
| Low | Anonymous blog, social media, marketing material, unverified claim |
| Unknown | Cannot determine authority |

### 2. Relevance
| Rating | Criterion |
|---|---|
| High | Directly answers a research question |
| Medium | Provides useful context or supporting data |
| Low | Tangentially related only |

### 3. Freshness
| Rating | Criterion |
|---|---|
| Current | Published within validity window for the topic |
| Possibly-stale | Published beyond typical validity but may still hold |
| Outdated | Superseded by newer information |
| Unknown | Cannot determine publication date |

### 4. Verifiability
| Rating | Criterion |
|---|---|
| High | Claims can be independently verified; data is accessible |
| Medium | Some claims verifiable; methodology described |
| Low | No methodology; no data access; assertion-only |

### 5. Bias Assessment
| Rating | Criterion |
|---|---|
| Low bias | Academic, official, or neutral source |
| Moderate bias | Industry source with declared interests |
| High bias | Marketing material, advocacy, or undisclosed interests |

### 6. Granularity
| Rating | Criterion |
|---|---|
| Sufficient | Provides specific data, methods, or details needed |
| Partial | Overview level; need to find more specific sources |
| Insufficient | Too abstract or high-level for research needs |

## Disqualification Criteria

A source should be excluded or flagged if:
- It contradicts multiple high-authority sources without explanation
- It has been retracted or officially corrected
- It is from a known predatory publisher
- It makes extraordinary claims without extraordinary evidence
- It cannot be attributed to any identifiable author or organization
