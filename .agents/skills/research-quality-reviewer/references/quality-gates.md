# Quality Gates for Research Reports

## Gate Dimensions

### 1. Question Alignment
Does the report address all research questions from the brief?
- Check each sub-question is answered or explicitly noted as unanswered
- Executive summary covers the core question

### 2. Evidence Completeness
Do key claims reference evidence entries?
- Every factual claim has `evidence_id`
- No "floating" claims without support
- PROPOSED items are clearly labeled

### 3. Citation Coverage
Are inline citations present for all factual statements?
- Findings section: every claim cited
- Analysis section: interpretations reference findings
- Exec summary: key claims cited

### 4. Logic Chain
No logical jumps between findings and recommendations?
- Each recommendation traces to findings
- Each finding traces to evidence
- No "therefore" without preceding support

### 5. Counter-evidence
Are contradicting sources discussed?
- Report acknowledges opposing views
- Contradictions from evidence ledger are addressed
- Not cherry-picking only supporting evidence

### 6. Method Fit
Is the synthesis method appropriate?
- Method matches research type
- Method is described in methodology section
- Limitations of chosen method acknowledged

### 7. Actionability
Do recommendations include priority and conditions?
- Each recommendation has priority level
- Conditions/context for applicability stated
- Not just "should do X" without when/how/why

### 8. Expression Quality
No unsupported superlatives or vague claims?
- No "increasingly important" without data
- No "widely recognized" without citation
- No "comprehensive and robust" as filler
- Specific over general

### 9. Risk Disclosure
Are limitations and data freshness covered?
- Limitations section present
- Data freshness noted
- Sample size / coverage gaps acknowledged
- Methodology limitations stated

## Grading

| Grade | Criteria |
|---|---|
| A | All 9 dimensions pass |
| B | 7-8 pass, remaining are partial |
| C | 5-6 pass, blocking issues exist |
| D | <5 pass, major revision needed |
| F | Fundamental problems (no evidence, no question) |

## Blocking Issues

A report CANNOT be published if:
- Key claims lack evidence references
- No counter-evidence is discussed
- Executive summary contains unsupported claims
- Research questions from brief are not addressed
